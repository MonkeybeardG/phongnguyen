Phong's Viet Cook Book
​

My parents were born in Vietnam, so I come from a family full of heritage which means we eat Vietnamese food daily. I always noticed that online, its much harder to find real authentic Asian recipes in English so I wanted to document some of my family's favorite recipes. But just to be clear, these recipes are far from an exact science. A lot of Asian Cooking is based off feeling. In my household, you will hardly ever see my mom or aunt pull out a measuring cup. Instead they eyeball and somehow the food always turns out good. Anyways heres a couple recipes to look at. P.S. this cook book is not done and I am still adding to it whenever I can.

Phở Gà
Phở is probably the most well known Vietnamese food and is great introduction to the cuisine. For a chicken soup, you would be surprised how light a bowl of Phở can be. Phở is great. I can confidently say we eat Phở at-least once a week. The way my family makes Phở is sorta healthy, In general we tend to substitute unhealthy ingredients. For example, instead of using rock sugar my aunt uses apples in Phở.
Stock:
  • 3-4 onions ( cuts made inside to help release flavor)
  • 5 large carrots peeled and chopped into large chunks 
  • 4-5 apples (any apple but Granny Smith apple) cut in half (we use Gala apple)
  • 4 ginger cut horizontally into chunks
  • 1 whole chicken (preferably Asian chicken)
  • 1 Tsp dried Fennel 
  • 1 Tsp coriander
  • 7 Star anise
  • 2 sticks cinnamon 
  • 5 Clove
  • 10 black cardamom
  • 12 quarts of water
Garnish:
  • sliced jalapeños
  • roughly chopped cilantro
  • bean sprouts
  • sliced lemons
  • sliced white onion
Noodles:
  • any rice noodle
Directions:
  1. Roast herbs in a pan until aromatic and put in a tea bag
  2. Preseason raw chicken with chicken bouillon and salt. Be liberal
  3. Put onions carrots apples ginger and chicken in boiling water.
  4. When you put the ingredients in, the water will stop boiling. Let the water boil again then turn off the stove. Wait 30 minutes for the residual heat to cook the chicken.
  5. Take the chicken out and shred the meat.
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste the chicken.
  7. Put the bones and herbs back into the soup. Once boiling put the heat on low and simmer the soup for at least one hour (the longer the better). When cooking the soup for longer than an hour take the veggies and herbs out before continuing to simmer. To finish the soup add salt, chicken bouillon, and fish sauce to taste.
  8. Once broth is done, cook rice noodles in separate pot of boiling water to desired texture.
  9. Serve noodles with soup, bean sprouts, lemon, Thai bay leaves, and shredded chicken.

Bánh Xèo​

Bánh Xèo is essentially a savory Vietnamese crepe. Its definitely a favorite in my household. Normally I will eat 4-5 in one sitting. It may look simple, but it's definitely challenging to make. Folding over the pancake takes a while to get a hang of and because the pan is so hot you have to work fast. I'm still a rookie when it comes to Bánh Xèo. Anyways heres my families version of Bánh Xèo. Hope you enjoy
Batter:
  • 1 bag of rice flour and 1 bowl of rice leftover to grind and pour into rice flour
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric powders 
  • 1 teaspoon salt 
  • 3 scallions, thinly sliced
  • 5 cups of water 
Filling:
  • 8 ounces shrimp,turkey bacon 
  • 1 bag of bean sprouts
Garnish:
  • Thai basil
  • Romaine lettuce
Dipping sauce:
  • 1/2 cup fish sauce
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 to 3 Thai chiles finely chopped
  • 1 to 2 cloves garlic finely copped
Directions:
  1. For the batter, whisk together the all-purpose flour, rice flour, turmeric, green onion, grinding cooked rice and salt in a large bowl and Let rest for 15 to 30 minutes.
  2. Heat nonstick pan on medium-high heat for about 2 minutes
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of the oil, 3 to 5 slices of turkey bacon and 3 to 5 pieces of shrimp.
  4. Sauté until the turkey bacon is light brown and the shrimp are half cooked, 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Pour 1/2 cup into the hot skillet, swirling to coat the bottom.
  6. Top with a small handful of bean sprouts.
  7. Cook until batter looks set and edges start to brown, about 1 minute.
  8. Fold Bánh Xèo over and slide onto an oven-safe plate.
